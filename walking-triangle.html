The walker is a continuous mesh in two stacked sections — a TORSO above and HIPS below — sharing a four-vertex mid-ring at z = torsoZ. Eleven vertices, fourteen faces total. The torso is a rigid box (top quad, four side quads, with the mid-ring as its bottom). The hips wedge from the mid-ring down through the crotch (z = crotchZ) and out to the two feet. The mid-ring belongs to both sections; the seam-faces between them are internal and are not rendered. Two parameters tune the silhouette: torsoZ sets where the seam sits, and midScale sets the mid-ring's footprint relative to the top — 1.0 matches the top exactly (a boxy torso), < 1 tapers the body inward at the waist, > 1 flares the hips wider than the shoulders.

The dominant parameter that distinguishes gaits is the stance fraction f — what fraction of each cycle a foot spends planted. Walking has f > 0.5 (a brief double-support overlap as one foot lands before the other lifts). Running has f < 0.5 (a flight phase between stances). Shuffling pushes f high (lots of double-support, low foot lift). The no-slip math generalizes: foot's local-x amplitude A = v·T·f / 2, and the track angular rate becomes ω_track = A·ω_phase / (π·R·f). Each gait also brings its own multipliers for cadence, stride, lift, bob, and a forward-lean offset; expression axes compose with these on top, so a weary run is genuinely slower and lower than a proud run, while still reading as running.

Order of operations matters, and now there are two pivots. First, walking deforms the feet in the local frame. Then the torso rotates around the mid-ring centroid (its own pitch and roll, posture- and composure-driven). Then the hips rotate around the walker origin (gait-driven roll and pitch, plus slow wobble). Then a whole-body bob translate, then yaw, then track translate, and finally projection. The torso's roll oscillates with the gait but is phase-lagged by π — when one hip drops, the torso rolls toward the planted foot, the human counter-rotation between shoulders and hips that gives a walk much of its life. Faces are then backface-culled, depth-sorted along the eye direction, and painted back-to-front, each shaded by its world-space normal dotted with a soft overhead light.

Toggle footprints to witness no-slip directly: each plant drops a mark at the foot's world position, and the walker glides past leaving a tidy trail along the track. Toggle axes for the coordinate triads — black at world origin, terracotta riding with the body. The projection is symmetric 30° iso, so the world circle becomes an axis-aligned ellipse with semi-axes R√(3/2) horizontally and R√(1/2) vertically.

The expression panel exposes four orthogonal axes — energy, posture, composure, stance — each modulating multiple gait constants at once. Energy scales cadence, stride, lift, and bob magnitude together; the no-slip relation ω_track = 2A·ω_phase / πR keeps feet planted as stride changes. Posture lifts or sinks the body each step (asymmetric bob bias) and tilts the rest pose forward or back. Composure inversely scales transient roll and pitch — at low composure a slow drift in roll and pitch is layered on top, like a wobble at the edge of balance. Stance widens or narrows the half-width baseW at the level of the rest pose, so footprints land farther apart or closer together. Named presets — happy, sad, proud, angry, drunk, sneaking, weary, anxious — are simply named points in this four-dimensional space.
